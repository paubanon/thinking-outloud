Social media is a bottomless pit. I don't mean that as a metaphor. I mean it pretty literally.

They're designed so you never reach the bottom. The infinite scroll, the notifications, the likes, the algorithms that show you exactly the type of content most likely to keep you hooked. Everything is built to maximize the time you spend inside.

It's not an accident. It's the business model. Your attention is the product they sell to advertisers.

And the problem is that attention is a finite resource. Every minute you spend on Instagram or Twitter is a minute you're not doing something else. And if that minute multiplies into hours a day, over years, the impact is enormous.

It's not that looking at cat photos is bad in itself. The problem is the scale and the way it consumes something that's very valuable: your ability to concentrate and your time.

There are studies suggesting that excessive social media use is correlated with higher levels of anxiety and depression, especially in teenagers. And it makes sense. You're constantly exposing yourself to an edited and magnified version of other people's lives, comparing yourself, looking for validation in the form of likes.

What I'm proposing is not necessarily to get rid of them, though for some people that might be the right option. What I'm proposing is to be conscious. Use them with intention, not by default. Ask yourself: am I opening this because I want something specific, or just because it's what I do when I don't know what to do with my hands?

Set limits. Disable notifications. Don't check them first thing in the morning or last thing at night. Try going a day without them and see how you feel.

Attention is one of the most valuable things you have. Protect it.
