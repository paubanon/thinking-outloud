I don't think you'll be able to see anything because there's not much light on German streets, but I'll try.

About what I was saying earlier on stress, and how almost all the remedies or methods for improving, that is, for living a better life, are based on stressing us out so we learn to control it. A controlled stress that allows us to improve.

What basically happens is that people have gone soft. They're living in hyper-comfort and they're not aware that what we actually like, what humans really like, isn't being comfortable. What we like is having control, having the power to say, I do what I want, I know I can do what I want. And you only learn that by exposing yourself again and again and again to stress, controlling it and managing it.

So in today's society where everything is hyper-comfortable, the only stress that reaches us — because it's the only kind that does — is the one from work. And that's a stress that's hard to adapt to because it's not a sudden stress. Human beings adapt and grow when we're given a spike of stress and then have time to recover. A fight and a rest, a sprint and a rest.

When what you have is a dose of stress that isn't extremely high but is constant, that destroys you. You have to learn to manage it so that small dose of stress isn't stress, but your baseline. I now with university know there are people who would say, man, I have so much work, screw this. But I don't take it as stress.

I've always said stress is optional. The same situation for two different people can be super stressful or it can not be. For example, imagine someone has a job and in that job they have a lot of things to do, they have their responsibilities, and they're stressed. They say, oh damn, I just can't take it anymore, the job... Okay, now take another person who was unemployed and gets that job. The same job, with the same responsibilities, everything identical. The only difference is their mindset, the way they approach this stress isn't: ugh, I have so many things to do, what a nightmare! But rather: look, great that I have a job, I'm happy, finally. And the same job feels stress-free.

So what we have to cultivate is this: learning to deal with stress, managing it, having a mindset that, even when we have things to do, doesn't stress us out.
