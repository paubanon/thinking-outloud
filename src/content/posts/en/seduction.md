I don't think I'm where I'd like to be yet in this area, but I've still come a long way and I'm sure I can contribute something to people who haven't come as far. The topic is seduction, and it's a hard one to talk about because there's a lot of taboo, a lot of shame, a lot of fear, and also a lot of people who don't like it because they think some of the views can be sexist or similar. It's a very complex topic. I'm going to do my best because the other option is not making the video and putting it off for years.

First: I don't consider myself any kind of master, or pick-up artist, or anything like that. I've failed many times, I still have many things I want to improve on, I'm still learning. But looking at where I was and where I am now, I've improved and changed a lot.

Why study seduction? It tends to get a really bad rap because it's interpreted as something that has to come naturally. For many of us that's not the case. There are people who are very good socially, great. But the advice of "if you're introverted, accept it" is a useless piece of advice, because it ignores the fact that for most introverted people, being introverted doesn't mean being happy being that way. What usually happens is that you're introverted, you don't like it because you'd like to socialize more, but you're more afraid of learning to be more outgoing than the discomfort that comes with staying introverted. So you just end up with the worst of both. That causes frustration.

The things that scare us are a neon sign telling us: there's work to be done here. And that was kind of why I started studying. Identifying that I wasn't comfortable in that area of my life. I wasn't comfortable starting conversations with women, opening up a conversation, making an interaction sexual, going in for a kiss. There was nothing conscious about that process. The revelation came one day from my dad, who told me: you don't need to study for that. And I started thinking and said: maybe that's not true. Maybe there's no area where knowing other people's experiences isn't valuable to you. Doesn't matter if it's seduction, meditation, cold exposure, whatever. It's always good to know others' experiences and then you decide what to do with them.

A lot of people have objections: I don't want to manipulate people, this has to come naturally. Those are excuses. Excuses to avoid facing an area we're afraid of. My opinion: read. Read about seduction. Read a lot. If you're genuinely watching this video, it's because there's a small part of you that says this needs to be worked on. There will be terrible advice. There will be misogynistic opinions. That's what critical judgment is for. You're not going to apply everything you read. You have to read and decide what to apply. Not acquiring knowledge because you're afraid that knowledge will damage you is stupid. It's like not studying Nazism because you're afraid of becoming a Nazi.

I started because it was an area where I had no control. I have control over my training, my diet, my studies. But this was an area where I had no control and I decided it needed to be worked on. And I think like me, there are many men who are afraid to talk to women, to show romantic or sexual interest. It's not healthy to live with that fear inside you forever. If you have a fear, the thing to do is deal with it.

There's a very decisive point I want to make clear: for me seduction is not a process of flirting more. For me seduction is a process of acceptance, of overcoming fears, of accepting your sexuality, of taking control over this area of your life. That will obviously lead to having more interactions and more encounters, but above all it will lead to knowing yourself better, to feeling more comfortable with yourself, to having more self-confidence. This is not about how many women you sleep with. It's about how you improve yourself.

It's also important to know that the progress is not linear. It's more like a sigmoid function: zero until it reaches a certain value and then it suddenly spikes. This means that until you're extremely good your numbers won't go up even though you've made a lot of progress. You can work a lot and still not see results until much later. Keep that in mind so you don't lose motivation.

For me the key to seduction, and how the authors I've liked the most understand it too, is honesty. A lot of the advice given in the seduction community involves cold reading methods, neurolinguistic programming, subliminal messages, hiding intentions. All of that feels false to me. To be comfortable with yourself you have to be honest, first with yourself and then with the other person. Those are Band-Aids. And I don't like Band-Aids.

A fundamental point, and I underline it: eliminating pornography from your life. I've been working on this for over a year now and it's hard, very hard, and I have a whole video to make about it. But it's a radical change in how your brain works. Dopamine is the neurotransmitter responsible for motivating you. Sex is what generates the most dopamine, by a wide margin. When you watch porn you're exposing your brain to the most attractive women on the planet in complete sexual availability for you and in whatever quantities you want. That causes brutal dopamine spikes that desensitize your brain. Everyday life starts to feel less interesting. You enjoy music less, you have less motivation to study, to work. And above all, your brain says: why would I go talk to that girl at the café when I can go home and watch three porn videos? When you remove the pornography, your brain suddenly says: either we start moving or we don't get laid.

On the other hand, pornography creates unrealistic standards. The women in your daily life will seem less attractive to you because they're going to be real people with flaws. For normal people to seem attractive to you, it's necessary to calibrate your standards to real life. It's not about lowering your standards. It's about expanding them beyond physical attraction. When you eliminate pornography you start including in what attracts you whether the person is smart, whether she's fun, whether she has something interesting to contribute. And the attraction process itself becomes much more enjoyable.

When I stopped porn and masturbation I realized that before I had been interacting with this background chip of: you're doing all this so that afterward you can sleep with her. When you stop, that changes. You're having coffee, looking into each other's eyes, flirting, and you enjoy the mere act of flirting itself. That ends up changing your definition of success with women. Success is no longer getting them into bed. Success is being able to flirt, being comfortable, connecting emotionally with the other person at a much deeper level.

Alcohol is another topic. I gave that up too a while back, apart from the health implications, because I didn't want to only be able to interact with the opposite sex when I was tipsy or drunk. I wanted to be conscious of the fear and how I overcame it. Don't say: this scares me, I'm going to get high so I don't feel the fear. I want to feel the fear and know that I'm a person capable of facing it.

Book recommendations. In Spanish I have two and one in English. The first is *Sex Crack* by Mario Luna. Mario Luna strikes me as a pretty unpleasant person, very much a poser, and that caused me a strong resistance. But the book *Sex Crack* is an excellent guide for those of you who have no idea and want to give it a structure. It gives you a reasonable theoretical framework for what a romantic-sexual interaction is. The problem is that it's very oriented toward performing rather than creating value, and it shows.

The second is *El Seductor* by Luis Tejedor (alias Golán). Luis Tejedor is the only Spanish author, besides Mark Manson, who talks about being honest with women and honest with yourself. He talks about genuinely falling a little in love with each woman, about being direct, about being so comfortable with yourself that you're not ashamed of what you feel or what attracts you. It's incredibly satisfying to be able to tell a girl in a non-creepy way how attractive you find her and why. Telling her I like her hair not as a calculated compliment but because you genuinely feel that. And if you don't feel it, you don't say it.

The third is *Models* by Mark Manson, in English. It goes along the same lines: create value in your own life before giving it to others. Don't fake anything. If you do something, let it be honest.

And then there's the exposure. In seduction more than in any other area you have to get in there. You have to start talking to girls. And every time you see a girl who attracts you and you don't talk to her, think: damn. The best openers are situational. Don't look for weird stuff. The other day there was a girl reading a book, she'd been completely absorbed in it for an hour, I walked up to her and said: what are you reading, you've been in there for an hour? Something that simple. We ended up talking for half an hour and I left with her number.

You have to talk to girls in cafés, on the bus, at the gym, sometimes without any intention of flirting. Because it demystifies women and makes you a more outgoing person. And it's very unusual for you to get shut down if you come in naturally and normally. You get shut down if you use weird techniques or pick-up openers that are too aggressive.

Honesty from the start also removes the problem of the friendzone. If from minute one you're showing romantic-sexual interest, because you're being honest, you're not approaching to make friends. And if the other person says "I have a boyfriend but we could be friends," I say: no, I'm not interested in you as a friend. I was interested in you romantically and sexually. If you want we can exchange numbers and when you're free we'll see how things go. Because from the start you're honest. I like you and I tell you clearly, but I'm not in love with you and my life doesn't depend on you. If you say no, I say okay and I walk away. I like her, but if she's boring I'm also going to be the one who says goodbye.

And what I wanted to mention to wrap up is the transfer. Being honest in seduction leads you to being honest with yourself in everything else. You learn to empathize with your feelings, to be honest in your friendships, with your family, with your boss. It transfers to any kind of human relationship. And above all it helps you improve yourself as a person because you're working on an area that requires a lot of work, that is very scary, but that is very rewarding.

Seduction has been one of the pinnacle areas of my life. In the end, what do things revolve around: you have your family, your health, your work life, your friends, and your romantic-sexual life. Those are fundamental pillars. If one isn't going well and all the others are, things work, but ideally all of them require your attention. I have a friend who works at CERN and has told me that now his priority is no longer advancing at work but improving with women, because he notices it's something he needs in his life and that he's not paying attention to it. Working on that is extremely important.

I'm still on the path of learning an enormous amount, but I've learned a lot. I hope this was useful. For more specific guides there are the books I mentioned. Take care.
