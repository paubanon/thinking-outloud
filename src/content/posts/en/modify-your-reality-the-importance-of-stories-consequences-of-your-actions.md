Good morning. Today I wanted to share an example that just happened to me of how mindset affects your reality.

Yesterday I had things to get done in the morning — stuff for the company, for the university and so on. And when I went to bed yesterday I had two options: set an alarm, wake up early and get to work, or say no, sleep as much as you need. Each one has its thing. The point is I decided not to set an alarm because I value sleep a lot for cognitive function.

And today, when it came to getting up and getting to work, you have two options. Say, damn, I'm short on time because yesterday I was lazy and didn't set the alarm. Or say, no, you didn't set the alarm but you're much fresher because of it.

And it's an example of how the same fact — not setting the alarm and waking up later — you can take as a criticism of yourself and generate negative thoughts, or change the story you tell yourself and say: I chose to do this.

And learning to do that is a very important tool when it comes to day-to-day personal happiness. Faced with the same fact, shifting the mindset with which you approach it and saying: it's not that I was lazy yesterday and didn't set the alarm. I decided not to set the alarm, and even though I have less time to work now, I'm going to be more efficient because I'm more rested.

And the same with a thousand other situations. You can do it with relationships, with university, with work, with anything that might generate stress or discomfort. Try to flip it and say: I did this this way because I want to, because I decided to. Or, if I had no control and it went wrong, well — what's the point in torturing myself?

The story you tell yourself.
