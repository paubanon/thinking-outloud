I'm on my way to a meeting and I wanted to share a quick reflection on something that's happening to us a lot now. It's a side effect not of social media specifically, but of the ubiquity of technology in our lives: the need to over-confirm when you've made plans with someone, which basically translates into a lack of trust in the other person.

When you make plans with someone, the fact that they can be constantly connected creates the need to over-confirm. To say: "Hey, we agreed on half an hour! Right? Yeah? Hey, I'm leaving now! Come on!" It feels very strange to make plans with someone and just show up at the agreed place and time, without underlining that you have plans, that you're on your way, this and that.

It's happened to me many times. We'd agree on a Skype call at 7 in the evening. Since I'm someone very detached from my phone, I often have it off or in airplane mode, and at 7 I log into Skype. When I connect I see I have 3 or 4 messages from the other person at 6:45, at 6:50: "Hey, we agreed on fifteen minutes from now. You sure, right? Are you...?"

Why is it so hard for us to trust the other person? To trust they'll show up?

It's an interesting side effect, because it's not simply that people want to confirm and leave it at that. It's that they start to distrust the other person when they don't have constant feedback from them, precisely because the possibility of having that feedback exists. And this translates into personal relationships too: when a couple isn't constantly messaging each other, some people interpret it as a lack of trust or respect.

I think that's a mistake. To have a healthy relationship, whether with your partner or a friend, you need to not be writing each other constantly. The fact that you can be present all the time doesn't mean you have to be.
