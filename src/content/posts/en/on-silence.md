Today I want to talk about silence. Not the awkward silence that shows up when you don't know what to say, but that other silence — the one you choose. The one you seek out.

We live in a world that constantly bombards us with noise. Notifications, news, opinions, content. Everything competes for our attention, everything wants a piece of our time. And at some point we stopped asking ourselves whether we actually want to give that time.

I've realized that the best thoughts I've ever had came in moments of silence. Not meditating, not doing anything special. Just being. Walking without headphones, sitting on a bench without looking at my phone, cooking without a podcast in the background.

I'm not saying noise is bad. I'm saying silence has become something we have to actively seek out, when it used to be the natural state. Noise was the exception. Now it's the other way around.

And the funny thing is, when you find that silence, at first it's uncomfortable. Your mind wants to fill that space with something. But if you hold on a bit, if you let yourself be there without doing anything, ideas you didn't know you had start to surface. Connections you hadn't seen.

Silence isn't the absence of something. It's the presence of space. Space to think, to feel, to simply be.

I don't have an elaborate conclusion. I just wanted to share this. Sometimes the simplest things are the ones we most need to hear.
