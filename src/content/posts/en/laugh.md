This is a fragment of the poem Alina, written by Hobbit Keutscherian. And what I actually wanted to talk to you about is just the first two, three lines. But there are things you can't leave unfinished.

The echo of your laughter will turn to flavors in your mouth. Sometimes vinegar, sometimes oil, sometimes sugar and sometimes salt. Laugh or freedom will become a cyst in the storage rooms of your heart. In its hollows, shadowy corners, you'll hide flavors, trembling, doubts, grudges, embraces, heartbreak, tears, illusions.

Today I wanted to talk a little about laughter. I don't know how many of you have paid attention to laughter. Or have thought about laughter itself. Laughter is a strange thing. Like almost everything that, when you look at it closely enough, no matter how mundane it is, becomes strange. We convulse slightly to communicate joy. And we do it involuntarily. And it gives us a lot of pleasure. It's very strange, laughing.

Not long ago I realized I wasn't laughing as much as I'd like. These past few months I've been pretty cooped up. Writing, thinking, finishing a master's degree. And dealing with some personal stuff. And all of it together, combined with social isolation, made me realize I wasn't laughing regularly.

I've always been someone who has a hard time laughing alone. I've never been able to laugh at a stand-up special. I've recognized it was funny and it struck me as clever. But I didn't laugh. Meanwhile I've sometimes heard people cracking up alone at home. I've never been able to do that. And I missed it a little. The colors. The spices. The flavor it gives to life.

I realized it and decided I wanted to laugh more. I decided I want a life with more laughter.

Some people think that when you meditate you take the fun out of life because you rationalize everything. And on one hand, that's true. You're very dominant with the prefrontal cortex. And that means that when you get angry, you don't let the anger take over. You're able to say: what's going on here? This doesn't run us. A friend told me once that he didn't want to start meditating because he was afraid of losing the spark of life. And while I have to say there's some truth to that — when you meditate a lot you live a calmer, more controlled life, very full, appreciating the details a lot — you have fewer ups and downs. That can be good or it can be bad.

In my case I try to minimize the lows and maximize the highs. And that means letting myself fall in love. Allowing myself to laugh a lot. Allowing myself to be irrational sometimes. And doing impulsive things that fill me with happiness even if they're not rational.

I decided, for starters, to pay attention to laughing. When do I laugh? Notice it. Enjoy the fact that you're laughing. Without breaking the laughter. And I ran my experiments. I tried doing a laughter session alone at home. I'm going to laugh right now, go. And the sensations were strange. Like I wasn't being fully serious doing the fake thing, but I also wasn't fully laughing. Somewhere in between. Maybe laughter is like tickling, like orgasms: that two is better than one.

And I started paying a lot of attention to when I laughed with people. And when I laughed, letting it all out. Without any shame.

How much do you laugh? How long has it been since you laughed? How long since you let out a big belly laugh? How long since you lost your voice because there was no air left in your lungs from laughing?

When you're with friends, with family, when something happens and laughter shows up, you don't force it, you don't think it, you don't plan it. Laughter appears. And sometimes laughter chains together. And you enter a flow state where you're not thinking about anything else, because what fills your consciousness in that moment is the joy of being in the present moment with who you're with. Doing what you're doing. Talking about what you're talking about. And it's one of the most beautiful moments in life.

I wanted to make this video to draw attention to those things. And to urge you, the next time you laugh, to know that you're laughing. And to appreciate it. Just like when you're at the top of a mountain you can say "how beautiful"... or you can really mean it. Appreciate it like that. There are people who can't laugh. There are people who don't laugh. There are sad people. There are people who suffer.

Laugh. Or else freedom will become a cyst in the storage rooms of your palate. A hug.
