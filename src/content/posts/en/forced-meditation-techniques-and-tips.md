Hey guys, I'm in the middle of the forest, it's night and it's dark, and I came here because I was scared, honestly. I was at home and my head was spinning a bit, and I decided to go for a walk. I've always wanted to come to the forest at night, but it scared me a lot, I won't lie. It's because the city ends and suddenly it's just you and an immeasurable darkness all around you. All of that is forest. And on top of that there's wind and it's making noise, it makes an incredible sound.

And it turns out it's made me think. I've taken it as the hero's journey: it scared me a lot, but you have to face the things that scare you, and among them is darkness, for biological reasons. I've been seeing the hero's journey as I walked. I kept going step by step. I realized I had a goal, reaching a viewpoint, and the thumbnail of this video is the photo from that viewpoint. And I realized that what you have to do is take it one step at a time. I'd reach a corner and say: fine, I'll make it to the next corner and then turn back. I'll make it to this path and then turn back. And bit by bit, I found the first viewpoint. I passed it and said: well, on to the next one. And I reached the second viewpoint and stood there. I turned off all the lights and my eyes adjusted to the darkness. Listen. My eyes adjusted to the darkness and it was fine. Actually I find it more uncomfortable right now filming this video, because I can only see myself and the flash, and everything else is darkness.

And I realized that there are certain emotions and certain thoughts that occupy your mind completely. These are what I call forced meditation. For me, forced meditation is those thoughts or emotions that arrive in your mind, set up camp there, and don't let anything else in, and don't go away.

I've found different ways to reach this state. Some are pleasant, some aren't.

You can reach this state with cold showers, which is something I really like. If you're overthinking something and you're tired, worried about university, anxious about something, get into a very cold bath. You can't think about absolutely anything except the cold.

Another is pain, obviously. I don't recommend that one, don't inflict pain on yourself if you can avoid it.

Another is fear, which is what I was experiencing here just now. I have some messages to reply to and honestly I felt incapable of picking up the phone and replying to them, because being alone in the middle of the forest at night required all my senses at a biological and evolutionary level.

And love is another one. There are moments when love takes over and the person you love is in your mind and you can't change that. You can't remove it, you can't think about anything else. For better and for worse. There are moments when you're overwhelmed with love. And there are moments when love strikes with its double-edged sword.

It's in those moments that it's useful to have the rest of the forced meditation tools. If you're thinking about your girlfriend, about worries over university, go do something that literally prevents you from thinking about anything else. You can also learn not to think about it, which is what meditation is for. But if you want to force yourself not to think, try some of those therapies like cold exposure and so on.

Meditation itself also helps you identify these moments and enjoy them more.

Face them, enjoy these moments. Keep these techniques in mind if they help. And face what scares you. First, acknowledge it. Acknowledge that coming to the forest at night scares you. And second, face it. And face it like the warrior who has to go rescue the princess. Face the fear directly, head-on and voluntarily. It's better to go after the dragon in its cave before it comes for you. Because then you'll come out, if you come out, stronger. And it turns out it's easier to defeat the dragon when you go for it instead of waiting for it to come for you. Good luck.
