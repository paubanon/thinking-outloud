Dostoyevsky wrote in Notes from Underground that if you give a human being everything they want — wealth, health, comfort, peace — sooner or later they'll go looking for chaos. Because human beings need to overcome obstacles. They need problems. If they don't have them, they create them.

Jordan Peterson talks about this a lot. The idea that a meaningful life isn't the most comfortable life, but the most loaded one. Loaded with responsibility, with challenge, with things to get through.

And this connects to something I think is really important: the problem isn't that you have problems. The problem is that you don't have the right problems. That you're wasting your capacity for suffering on things that don't deserve that suffering.

Because you're going to suffer either way. That's inevitable. The question is: what are you going to suffer for? Are you going to suffer because you don't know what show to watch tonight, or are you going to suffer because you're trying to build something that matters?

Seek out problems. But seek out the right ones.
