Miracles happen. But not if you stay home.

This phrase, which might sound like a cliché, holds something I think is a deep truth about how life works.

There are people who wait for things to happen. Who wait for the opportunity to knock on their door, for the love of their life to appear without them having to do anything, for the perfect career to materialize on its own. And while they wait, life passes.

And then there are people who move. Who go out, who expose themselves, who look, who ask, who fail, who try again. And things "happen" to those people that don't happen to the first kind.

It's not magic. It's probability. The more situations you put yourself in where something good can happen, the more likely something good is to happen.

This has very concrete implications. If you want to meet interesting people, you have to go to places where there are interesting people. If you want a professional opportunity, you have to put yourself out there, talk to people, show your work. If you want to learn something, you have to actively go looking for it, not wait for it to fall from the sky.

There's another element here too, which is asking. A lot of people don't ask for what they want. Out of fear of rejection, out of pride, out of not wanting to seem needy. And yet, most of the time the result of "not asking" is precisely not getting it.

What can you do today that moves you closer to something you want? Not tomorrow. Today.

It doesn't have to be something huge. It can be sending an email, talking to someone, signing up for something, publishing something you've created. Any movement in the right direction is better than standing still waiting.

Miracles happen. But they need a vector. They need you to be in motion to be able to reach you.
