You delete it and go out to meet people. That's how you use Tinder to get more dates. Simple.

I know many of you will be surprised by the title. I'll continue the line from the previous seduction video and expand on it a little. If you're watching this video, I strongly recommend watching the previous one first because it's connected, and I think it's important to understand the philosophy from before to understand this one.

Why not use dating apps? I've used them for a long time, I have to say. And I'm sure if you're watching this video it's because you haven't had the results you expected. Neither did I. I think over several years of intermittent use I've met up with 23 people, and every single time it was much less interesting than expected. Spending all the time and logistics they require just to end up having a coffee with someone and saying: it was fine for a coffee but I'm not interested in you, you're not as active as you seemed, and you're definitely not as smart.

The point I'm arguing for in deleting Tinder is very simple. What kind of person do you want to attract and what kind of person do you want to be? I have my ideal of a woman. Is it within that ideal that the girl I want to have some kind of relationship with is on Tinder? Seriously — does a person with an interesting life need Tinder to meet someone? I try to picture it and say: no. That's not part of the ideal.

Set yourself the most archetypal ideals you can. James Bond, Iron Man, Conan. That person who embodies the ideals of what it means to be successful. Or if you don't want fictional characters, your own abstract ideal: someone responsible, who earns enough to live how they want. Think about it. Does that ideal have Tinder? Does he really need to delegate to an app to meet someone?

What happens with social media is that they exaggerate everything. They exaggerate permissiveness, they exaggerate vices. Tinder creates a very particular dynamic. There's a very high percentage of men compared to women, because this concept isn't as appealing to female psychology. Women are more selective. Evolutionarily that makes sense: since they can end up pregnant, they'd better make sure the person is of value, reliable, not going to disappear. That has resulted in them being much more selective. Men, on the other hand, like almost everything on Tinder. And the few likes women do give are matched 99% of the time.

What social dynamic results? The women on Tinder are going to be surrounded by drooling men. That means they're generally going to be little interested in being there, and do it out of pure boredom, looking for social approval. For a guy it means he's going to have to compete with hundreds of people, his chat has to be interesting enough for her to notice, and if the conversation dies, she doesn't care because she has 50 other chats going.

All that effort: photos taken with a professional camera or you don't stand out, a good bio, an opening line, texting skills, time, sometimes money. And all that is for the possibility of getting a match or two, with some perfectly average woman who doesn't even really know why she has Tinder. And on top of that you have to invest ten times more time and effort than if you just walked up to the most interesting woman you see at a bar and started talking to her.

What ideal do I want for myself? With everything I do I want someone athletic, who likes to take care of herself, who likes to read, who has ambitions. Do I seriously think that person is going to be on Tinder?

With the effort it takes to overcome your initial fear barrier and approach someone in person, you're already having an interaction ten times richer. Because there's eye contact, pauses, gestures, smell, appearance, intentionality, tone of voice. All of that is lost in a chat. Even just talking to one person a week, you're already having something much better than what you could potentially have with a Tinder match.

One of the moments that changed my thinking was when I made a fake account as if I were a woman, with some friends, just to see what it was like. The enormous amount of drooling men on there. And I said: do I really want to compete with all of these? Do I really want to be one more in this group? Instantly my brain said: no. That's not part of your ideal. Delete it. Stop lying to yourself. You're not meeting anyone on Tinder. Go out and meet real people.

And I'll also tell you that one of the two options builds character and personality, and the other doesn't. And I'm not going to tell you which one.
