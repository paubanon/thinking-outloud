Good morning. Okay, first of all I want to clarify that this is the second time I'm making this video because the first time I did it I looked a little dazed talking to the camera, I wasn't used to it yet, and it was dark, I had bags under my eyes, the video was a bit slow. Anyway, now that I've already recorded several videos, I want to redo it.

This video is basically an explanation of why I'm opening this channel, what to expect from the videos and the content and so on. First I want to introduce myself. My name is Pau Bañón, I was born in Elche, I studied four years of physics in Valencia. I graduated on time and then had a free year in which I started a company called Enso Movers. And right now I'm in Heidelberg, Germany, doing a master's in astrophysics.

By nature I've always been someone with a lot of mental restlessness — that sounds a bit like a condition. But basically I mean I'm someone who thinks about things a lot. Thinks everything over many times, even more times than necessary. Overthinks his life. I love philosophy, which also goes hand in hand with the degree. Physics has often been strongly inspired by or rooted in philosophy.

So I'm someone who loves thinking about every aspect of my life. About training, about my career, about my personal relationships, about cooking, about where I want to take my life, about why society is the way it is, about how social media influences us... In short, I overthink things.

I've spent a long time thinking about how to express all these ideas. I'd considered a blog, but after thinking it over I realized a blog doesn't let me express myself the way I like to express myself. I like to express myself like this, the way you see here. I start with something, with an idea I've turned over a little, and I develop it. I look at it from this angle, from this point, from this other one... Sometimes I stop developing that idea because another one has come up and I go off on a tangent, and I go down one thread, and then I come back, sometimes I don't come back, and those ideas stay half-formed.

And I think for that kind of erratic thinking, typical of the philosopher, YouTube is very interesting. Since several people have already told me they'd love to hear me talk and that they really enjoy it, I've decided to open this channel basically to use it as a diary, a video diary, a vlog.

It's simply a channel where I'll upload videos putting out thoughts about points of view, about philosophies of life. Sometimes I might explain an interesting concept from physics I've liked, other times I might reflect on social media, other times on how I approach life.

And I want to clarify that this channel isn't a... I don't want to be a YouTuber. That is, don't expect clickbait titles, don't expect edited videos, don't expect a YouTuber's tone of voice — which is worth a whole discussion, the typical YouTuber intonation. Expect videos like this one, of me talking about a topic.

If it interests you, stay and above all comment. I'd like people to comment or make response videos, I don't care. Because that motivates me to record more, gives me new ideas, I like being challenged, and anything I say is open to discussion, pushback, or thinking it over more and overthinking it further.

Since I don't want to be a YouTuber, I want you to know there won't be any super polished edited videos, and I'm not going to commit to any kind of schedule for uploading videos. There won't be a weekly video or a monthly video. It'll depend completely and absolutely on my life. On how much I feel like uploading videos, how inspired I am, and how busy I am. That means I might upload three videos in a week or I might go a month and only upload one.

In any case, I'd genuinely like to emphasize that if you like the content, if you identify with it, if you want to discuss, debate, whatever — I'll be happy to respond, to debate, to discuss. And if you live nearby and would like to meet up for a drink, that's much better than through social media, always.

I encourage everyone who can see this to interact. Not just watch the video, but really let it get your mind going a little — where it leads you to think, whether it was useful, whether it wasn't.

Ah, and I wanted to say one more thing: the kinds of thinking I'm going to put out here are often not closed lines of thought. Sometimes it will feel like I reach a point and just stop there. But it's because the videos aren't prepared with the intention of I'm going to teach people to do this. What I want is to stimulate you to think. And that means I might hand you a tangled ball of threads, and we start to unravel it, turn it over, think it through... And halfway through I say, okay, I'm done. And I leave it there.

Sometimes you'll notice that some videos are missing a final conclusion. Well, I'm sorry, but if it didn't come to me in that moment, there won't be one. Work it out yourselves, reach a conclusion, let me know. Just think of it as food for thought, as open questions of ethics, morality, our society today.

Let's see where we get to.
