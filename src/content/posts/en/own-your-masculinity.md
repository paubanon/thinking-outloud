The first point I'd like to cover in this series of videos on masculinity is the desire to let yourself be carried in relationships. Not too long ago, a friend who had just gone through a breakup asked me: "Man, can we ever just relax as men in a relationship? Can we ever just be at ease, at peace, comfortably with a person? Forget our problems and not have to be the ones driving things, leading? Can we ever just let ourselves go in a relationship, do we always have to be on?" Because he had suffered a breakup after a few years together.

And I would have loved to tell him yes, of course, man, that just means you had a girl who wasn't right and you'll find someone you can relax with and forget everything. But that wasn't my answer. My answer was that I don't think so. I think you can never take your partner for granted. Never. You're never going to be able to take a certain weight off yourself. A relationship is a project, and it's a project you can't forget. It won't move forward on its own. You're always going to have to put emphasis, care, attention into it. You're always going to have to carry a certain weight on your shoulders to keep it moving forward.

Reading the book The Way of the Superior Man by Deida, we could explain it this way. As you've seen in the previous video, a man has masculine and feminine energy, and so does a woman. But we have them in different amounts. By the way, when I talk about energy I don't mean literal energy, it's not a vibrating chakra. Talking about masculine and feminine energy is talking about masculine and feminine psychological archetypes that we both carry in our heads as a neurological pattern, driven by evolution. Since we came out of the water, we've been distilling what is masculine and what is feminine until arriving at very abstract archetypes. That's what I mean when I say energy.

Feminine energy is flow, it's life, it's impulsiveness, it's emotionality, it's surrender. Masculine energy is direction, leadership, will.

When you're in a relationship, it happens to all of us: you have the desire to let yourself go. To say: I can forget about this project now, I can relax with my partner and not have to think about keeping the spark alive. I don't want to carry the weight of the relationship, I don't want to be the pillar for the other person. All of that is your feminine energy as a man telling you: relax, let yourself go, it's fine.

And you'll have noticed that the moment you take your partner for granted, the moment she senses that you want to completely let go, her spark for you, her interest in you, disappears instantly. And it's something that confuses us men tremendously. The moment you tell a woman "I like you, I want to go out with you, I'm in love with you, forever and always," that's the fastest way you have to lose her. Unless you're at a very advanced stage of the relationship with concrete commitments. But even then, it's not something you want to say: it's something you want to show.

Why? A woman wants a man who is firm, solid in his convictions, who has direction. She wants a man with whom, when she's with him, she can feel completely safe, relaxed, protected. Who can shed all the pressures of day-to-day life, all the expectations society puts on her, and just be herself. Her feminine core, completely open and blooming. That's what a woman wants with you. And you have to be that person. You do it by embodying your energy of protector, of safety: I'm here and everything's fine, everything's okay, I've got this, so that she can bloom.

When you say "I want you forever, I need to relax one hundred percent, I don't want any weight on my back," you're embodying your feminine energy. She senses that if you can't carry that weight, if you can't embody that security, that pillar, she can't relax into her feminine energy and let herself go with you. And she loses all her interest. Because her interest, which emerges from her femininity, depends on you possessing your masculinity and embodying it.

So, as a man, you have to never take your partner for granted. Love her with everything you have, be willing to go all the way. But have your masculinity so solid that at any moment you could let it go if things weren't working out. That equanimity is fundamental. There's nothing more attractive than a man who is confident, who controls his desires and knows what he wants, with total detachment. In the case of a partner, what you want to convey is: I'm here one hundred percent, but if you tell me it's over I'm not going to go crying after you. I'll tell you I care about you, that I wouldn't want this to end. But I'm not going to be with someone who doesn't want to be with me. And you have to have made peace with that. And that has to be reflected in yourself.

It doesn't mean you don't commit. As a man I'd tell you to commit one hundred percent to your projects, to throw yourself into them. A relationship is one of those projects. You go all in until the end. But if the project is going badly, you're going to be able to close the box and move forward.

When meeting someone, it's the same. You want to meet a woman and want to tell her, not directly please, but show her: I'm interested in you sexually. I don't want a friend, I have plenty of those. I want you as a partner. And it's an offering you give her. You're inviting her to bloom with you, because you're a man who knows what he wants. You're telling her: I'm interested in you, I'd like to get to know you better and connect with you on a romantic level. But if you tell me no, that's fine. I'm not going to be offended, I'm not going to chase you.

Something I took from the book Models is to give it three attempts. You suggest meeting up. She can't. You suggest again. She can't. The third time, you say: look, I've tried to meet up three times now, and I genuinely would like to, but if you don't want to, just tell me and that's that. She might tell you you're right, that the reality is she's not interested in that way. And you as a man accept it and turn the page. But maybe she says: no, I really do want to meet up, let's meet on this day. And you do. They're not stupid, they're much more perceptive than us. They know perfectly well when a guy is playing the friend but actually likes them. And they know that's acting in a cowardly way, not saying what you want because you're afraid of rejection. That's not attractive.

I think this is a good starting point for understanding masculinity in this series. Understanding that those energies exist and why, when you chase a woman, her interest in you disappears. Both in a relationship and in seduction. I strongly recommend the book The Way of the Superior Man, once you read it you'll feel like: I knew all of this somehow inside me, but I wasn't aware of it. Once you understand it, all relationships become much more fun and much healthier.
