I want to talk about the relationship between responsibility and health, because I think they're concepts that are much more connected than they seem.

Responsibility is basically the capacity to respond. To respond to your life, to your circumstances, to what happens to you. Not in the sense of blame, but in the sense of agency. You have the capacity to influence what happens to you.

And in the area of health, this is especially clear. The vast majority of health problems people have in the West today are preventable. They're a direct consequence of decisions: what you eat, how much you sleep, whether you exercise or not, whether you smoke, whether you drink in excess. These are decisions people make, and they have consequences.

I'm not saying everything is each person's fault. There are genetic diseases, there are accidents, there are things that are outside anyone's control. But an enormous portion of the health problems we see are avoidable.

And yet the dominant narrative tends to externalize responsibility. It's the fault of the food industry, it's the fault of the system, it's the fault of work stress. All of that may be part of the context, but ultimately you're the one who decides what you put in your mouth and whether you move or not.

What I find interesting about thinking about health from the standpoint of responsibility is that it gives you back control. If the problem is external, you can't do anything. If the problem is partly in your own decisions, you can.

Eating well, sleeping well, exercising regularly, not smoking, moderating alcohol. These four or five things, if you do them consistently over years, make an enormous difference. Not just aesthetically, but in how you feel, in your energy, in your mental clarity, in your mood.

Health is one of the pillars that everything else rests on. It's hard to do anything else well if your body and mind aren't in shape. It has to be a priority.

And taking responsibility for your health isn't a burden. It's the opposite. It's a way of telling yourself: I have influence over how I feel. And that's liberating.
