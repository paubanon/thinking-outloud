Today I want to talk about fear. The fear of relationships and the fear of the future, which I think are two versions of the same basic fear: the fear of uncertainty.

Relationships are scary because you can't control them. You can't know how it's going to turn out, whether the other person will stick around, whether they'll hurt you, whether you'll hurt them. There's an intrinsic vulnerability in any real relationship.

And the future is scary for the same reason. You don't know what's going to happen. You don't know whether the decisions you're making now are going to turn out to be the right ones. You don't know what kind of person you'll be in ten years.

A lot of people respond to that fear by avoiding. Avoiding committing to relationships so they don't get hurt. Avoiding making important decisions so they don't make a mistake. Living in a kind of safe limbo where nothing bad can happen, but nothing good can either.

And that's a trap. Because life doesn't leave you in limbo indefinitely. Time passes anyway. And if you're not actively choosing, you're letting circumstances choose for you.

What I've learned, or am learning, is that the fear doesn't go away. There's no point at which you suddenly stop being afraid and become brave. What happens is that you learn to act in spite of the fear. Which are two very different things.

Acting in spite of fear means saying: I know this is scary, I know it might go wrong, and I'm doing it anyway. Not because it's reckless, but because the cost of not doing it, the cost of staying paralyzed, is greater than the cost of trying and failing.

With relationships, this means being willing to put yourself out there. To be vulnerable. To say what you feel even when you're not sure how it's going to land.

With the future, it means making decisions with the information you have now, knowing you'll never have all the information, and trusting in your ability to adapt when things don't go as expected. Which they won't. They never go exactly as expected.

And that's the message: don't wait until you're not afraid to live. Live with the fear. It comes with the package.
