I think part of why I arrived here and felt at home, after feeling a bit off before coming to the house, is because I had my things: my clothes, my bed, my bike, my TV, my little trinkets stuck here and there, my candles, my posters on the wall. And having things, yes, it ties you to them, but it also gives you warmth and a sense of home. My things. What makes your home different from any home? Your bike by the lamp, your stuff, your smell. All of that.

And I think the same thing happens with personal relationships. A lot of people are afraid to have personal relationships, to get involved with people, because it's a burden. And it's true that there are moments when it isn't easy, when you have to put in effort, when there are times they're weak points that can make you suffer. If you have things and you lose them, you suffer. If you have relationships and that person lets you down, you suffer. But the alternative is a flat life. A life in black and white, without peaks, without highs or lows, without love or hate. It's avoiding the hard moments at the cost of not enjoying it to the fullest.

That's why I think you have to cultivate personal relationships, get involved with people, love. Love hard. I'm in love with life and with people. And even though it's true that, as I said, you can suffer, I think it pays dividends. And it's the best thing we have in this life: having people around us who love us, who we can trust, who we can talk to, knowing they're there.

And they'll let you down, of course, on both sides. You won't always be winning. You have to put your part in. That's why relationships require time and effort from both sides to grow.

I think a minimalist perspective on relationships — of saying, I'm not going to have serious relationships with people, the typical fear of commitment, if you're dating and it lasts a month, well, just a month, that's how it is today and fine — that euphemism for trying to keep everyone at arm's length, I don't like. And it's to avoid the responsibilities that come with a relationship.

Because of course it has its good things. Not just romantic relationships, but with friends, with family. Saying: I'm going to spend time with this friend, I think this person is going to bring something to my life, I'm not going to say "let's see what time brings," I'm going to build a good relationship. I'm going to forge a good relationship with my family, I'm going to talk to them, I'm going to help them, I'm going to listen to them when they need it.

Yes, as I said, it puts a certain weight on your shoulders. But for everything it gives you, for all the love and wellbeing it gives you, it's a weight that becomes light.
