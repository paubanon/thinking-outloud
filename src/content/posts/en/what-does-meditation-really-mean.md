Hi everyone, good morning. I just finished my meditation session, I'm taking a walk, and something came to me that I wanted to share — for those of you who already meditate, it might serve as a good indicator that you've reached a certain level and you're on the right track. And for those who don't meditate, to give you a picture of what it means and the changes that meditating produces.

Two examples. One is whether you can keep your mind calm even on nights when it won't stop talking. If on those sleepless nights, because your head won't stop worrying about today, tomorrow, what's going on — you're able to say: brain, it's okay, we'll look at it tomorrow. And you're able to set that aside and focus on resting — you're on a very good path.

And another good indicator is if a song gets stuck in your head, that thing you're humming all day, and you're able to notice that you have that song in your head and let it go without it coming back. It also means you're on the right track.

And for those who don't meditate — meditation is that. Noticing something. Noticing that you have a song in your head, or noticing that you're having certain thoughts. Being aware that you're thinking that and having the capacity to decide what to put your attention on and keep it there. That's it.
