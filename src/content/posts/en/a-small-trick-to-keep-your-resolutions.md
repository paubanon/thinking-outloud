Today I shared a post on Instagram about the problems that porn consumption causes, especially prolonged consumption, on the neural reward circuits and dopamine and so on. And I thought you might like one of the methods I like most when you want to quit some kind of addiction or make a change and it's hard, it's hard and it sucks, but you know you have to do it.

The kind of thing like: I want to start training. I want to start doing long fasts. I want to quit porn. These are very primal instincts and even though you don't want them rationally, emotionally it's going to be hard. You're going to have moments where you say: I feel like doing it anyway, or I want to eat, I said I was going to do a 24-hour fast but I want to eat.

What I do is: when the urge hits me, whether for good or for bad — I want to eat and I'm supposed to be fasting, or I don't want to train and that impulse hits me emotionally — I go and look up information about it. Information about why you decided to do that. If you're fasting and the urge to break it hits you, you search: benefits of fasting. You look it up, read a little, and when you come back to it you understand again why you decided to do that. It's a very easy way to get through the impulse.

It can be applied to a thousand things. Cutting out sugar: when you're about to put sugar in something, part of your brain is going to say "one day won't hurt" and another part is going to say don't do it. What you have to do is: pause. Harms of sugar. Benefits of cutting out sugar. Why I decided to go without sugar. What my goals are. The moment you see rational information about it, the emotional impulse to do what you decided not to do disappears.
