Hey guys, it's been a long time since I made a walking video, but Spain lends itself more to this kind of thing. This is kind of an update video. It's been a while since I uploaded one and, well, some things have happened in my life that I want to reflect on.

Among them, and the main reason I haven't uploaded videos, is that I was seeing a girl and we broke up about a week and a bit ago. And it's honestly taken away some of my drive to do things. It's what I've mentioned before: when you're down, you have to accept that you're down and try to get out. What you can't do is wallow in the mud. So I accepted that I had little motivation to work, to go out, to do things.

And it's come together with the fact that here in Spain I'm on vacation, but all my social circles are working, studying, prepping for civil service exams and similar. Which makes it very difficult, if not impossible, to make plans. And it leaves me with a lot of free time. And I start climbing the walls a little.

So I want to make a self-referential nod to a video I made a while back called "Miracles exist, but not if you stay home." Because I've reached the same conclusion again. I run this channel to reflect and work through thoughts. So some of the thoughts I have I need to rethink and apply to myself from time to time.

I've realized that I want to be productive, but that's not always possible. And especially not possible when I'm at home. That doesn't happen. However, I've realized that I crave social contact. So if instead of staying home I get up and do the same things I'd do at home — reading, working on the laptop, that kind of stuff — but in a café, I'm so, so, so much more productive. And I feel better. Because I have the feeling that I've socialized, even if I haven't talked to anyone. But it's not just that: you open the door to the possibility of socializing. Of talking to the barista. Of running into a friend. Of meeting random people because they sit next to you. And all of that enriches your day-to-day experience.

And now that I don't have a partner, I've started moving again. Let's put it that way. Going out for a drink, even just a herbal tea, a coffee, whatever, at a café, already opens the door to a thousand things you couldn't do if you stayed home.

And today I finally woke up wanting to take on the world. To start new projects. To sit down, work. And end the morning exhausted going: I did this, I did this, I did this. I helped my mom with this. I walked the dog.

So you have to get your ass moving. It's the only way. Never stop moving.

And when you're down, accept that you're down and try to push forward. I knew, for example, that after this breakup it would take me some time to adjust. But I knew I was going to come out of it. I knew this couldn't last forever. And I started doing things to the extent that I felt motivated. Not as many came out as I wanted, which frustrated me a little. And finally the motivation to work, to push, starts coming back.

And today, Saturday, after a week and a half, I wake up again feeling like I have wings, ready to take on the world. I have to start this project, this project. Push Submovers hard. I want to make a video. I want to write about this. I want to tweak my training program. And that's my real self. And what you have to be clear on is which self is your real self. Which self do you want to give fire to. Because miracles exist. But not if you stay home.
