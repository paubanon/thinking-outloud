Why are you just sitting there?

I don't mean it as a criticism. I mean it as a genuine question. What are you waiting for?

There are things you know you need to do. There are projects that have been living in your head for months. There are conversations you've been avoiding. There are habits you want to change. What's going on?

The answer is usually some version of "now's not the right time." When summer ends. When the new year starts. When I have more money. When I'm more prepared. When I feel motivated.

And meanwhile, time passes.

Motivation is a trap. Most people wait to feel motivated before they act. But motivation doesn't work that way. Motivation comes after acting, not before. You start doing something, anything, and the momentum starts working in your favor.

The right question isn't "do I feel motivated to do this?" but "do I need to do it?" If the answer is yes, do it now. Not tomorrow. Now.

This applies to almost everything. Exercise. Work. Difficult conversations. Creative projects. The perfect moment never comes. The perfect moment is now, with all the imperfection that implies.

There's a concept called *activation energy*. In chemistry it's the minimum energy needed for a reaction to start. In personal life it's the initial effort it takes to begin something. And it's always the biggest obstacle.

Once you start, things flow much better. The problem is starting.

So: get up. Start. Five minutes now is worth more than perfect plans for tomorrow.

Why are you just sitting there?
