As a follow-up to the previous video, I just want to say that a lot of people do exactly this: they jump from teacher to teacher. And I think these people suffer from a lack of their own thinking. Because if you keep jumping from teacher to teacher looking for answers, you never give yourself time to sit with the questions. Or you never give yourself time to come up with answers different from what the teacher gives you.

In other words: you only appreciate a good training program, for example, when you've been through the difficulty of organizing your own training yourself. When you sat down and did it yourself. You saw how hard it is to put together. Then, when you see someone else's program, you think: wow, this is really good.

I think it's important to have teachers, I think it's important to have good teachers, but I think it's important for there to be periods of autonomous learning. Very important for personal development. The absence of teachers stimulates you, forces you to find the answer on your own, to work it out, to maybe even change the question. Maybe the question you were asking wasn't the right question, which is why you weren't finding a solution. Maybe what needs to change is the question.

I think that's something you can't learn regardless of how many teachers you have. It's a path you have to walk alone.
