This is a response video to Álvaro 2.0's video, who was responding to my video on improvisation, communication, and socializing. Because there was a point in it that gave me something to talk about, which is the moment when you say: you have to know how to flirt. With women, that's beside the point. You have to know how to flirt with everyone. And what do we mean by that? That you have to know how to interact with people, you have to understand them to get along with them. Because that's going to open an enormous number of doors for you throughout your life.

He already mentions it in the video, in the friend group we have. The dynamic we're trying to cultivate is: any chance for improvement in any area of your life, improve it. If you can get better, you do. So, an area where I, for example — I'm not going to speak for anyone else — had not been satisfied was the way I related to women. I was nervous, I didn't really know how to approach them. And I think 90% of men have this problem, or have been through it at some point in their lives.

The difference between many men and others is that some of us say: with this... this is not the self I want to be. I don't want to be a held-back version of myself. I want to know how to flirt, I don't want this shyness. This is a drag, to me. Going out into the world and feeling too embarrassed to talk to someone spontaneously. I wasn't Steve Urkel or anything, but I had less control, less spontaneity and fluency than I would have liked.

And besides the fact that everything improves with practice, it's also true that studying never hurts. Something I realized once when I was having lunch with my dad and he said: well, for that you don't need to study. And I sat there thinking: maybe you do. Maybe there is absolutely no area of your life where having extra knowledge, other people's experiences and their reflections, isn't a positive thing for you. Studying always helps you. Always. What you've studied, you decide whether to keep it or throw it out. But you already know that that field of knowledge... you already know it doesn't serve you. That's what it served you for.

And it's the same with flirting. And why not learn? I got into it, into studying. There are different euphemisms instead of saying flirting: you can say you were studying human relations between men and women, or that you were learning to socialize. However you want to see it.

The thing is that in the end it turns out that flirting, seduction, is very different from what people think it is. People think it's manipulative techniques to trick the other person and get them into bed. And the truth is that nothing could be further from the truth. 90% of what you learn is about changing yourself — your way of thinking, your way of acting, how you handle rejection. And the remaining 10% are small communication tricks to make the other person more receptive to getting to know you. That's basically what seduction is.

And what Álvaro was saying is that when you've spent a bit of time in this world, you realize that those skills you've developed for seduction aren't only useful for flirting, but for seducing in many areas of your life. It's incredibly useful for finding a job — if you know how to seduce, you know how to present yourself correctly, you know how to show the attitude you want to show. That in a job interview is gold. It's useful when you have to give talks. It's useful when you have to ask someone a favor in a way that doesn't sound forced. You walk into a gas station, you have a flat tire, and you have to ask for help. Knowing seduction helps you in those situations.

So there's a point I'd like you to take away from this. One: learning something is never negative. Never. Sometimes we have this resistance of saying, oh, what if this gets in my head. If you have a strong enough mind, that doesn't happen. You can read Hitler's Mein Kampf and say, this guy was out of his mind. The text doesn't have the power to get in your head. You have to be the one who knows how to tell things apart. And when you have that power, you have the full spectrum of knowledge open to you, and there's no area of it that's negative. I've read books from which I've gotten three useful things and the rest is complete garbage I disagree with. Well, that's what it served me for.

There is absolutely no field in which learning doesn't serve you. Socializing, cooking, sex, training, eating. Don't take for granted that you know anything. Research it, question it, have a good critical mindset.

And second: give flirting a chance. Because it's a lot of fun, because you gain a lot of confidence, and because it's not manipulation. And because it's not just more fun for you — which it is. It's more fun for everyone. Because love is wonderful.

A hug and happy Christmas to everyone.
