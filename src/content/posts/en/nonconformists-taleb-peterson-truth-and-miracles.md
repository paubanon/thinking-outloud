Today I want to talk about a set of ideas that have been rattling around in my head, connected to nonconformism, Nassim Taleb, Jordan Peterson, truth, and what we might call miracles.

There's something that connects many of the people I admire most, and it's a kind of deep nonconformism. Not a surface-level "I'm not like other people" nonconformism, but one that comes from actually thinking things through and refusing to accept an idea just because everyone else does.

Nassim Taleb is a perfect example. Taleb has a way of seeing the world that collides head-on with academic consensus and conventional wisdom. His ideas about fragility, risk, and uncertainty are deeply counterintuitive. And yet, when you hear them, you feel there's something true in them that conventional thinking has ignored.

Jordan Peterson has something similar. Whatever your opinion of him, you have to admit he's a thinker who doesn't fit comfortably into any category. And that makes a lot of people uncomfortable.

What interests me about this is the relationship to truth. Truth, in many cases, is nonconformist by nature. Because truth owes nothing to what the majority believes or what's socially convenient to say. And people who are committed to truth over consensus are, almost by definition, nonconformists.

Then there are miracles. I use the word without necessarily religious connotations. I mean those things that seem impossible but happen — those coincidences that are too perfect to be just coincidences. And what I find interesting is that miracles, understood this way, don't happen if you don't put yourself out there. They don't happen if you stay home.

There's an idea I really like: luck favors those who put themselves in situations where luck can happen. Miracles don't visit people who are hiding.

This connects to everything above. Nonconformists — people who think independently, who put themselves out there, who seek truth even when it's uncomfortable — are also the people for whom more "miracles" happen. Not by magic, but because they're active, they're moving, they're willing to be surprised.

And that's what I want to be. An active person, exposed, willing to change my mind when the evidence justifies it, and committed to saying what I believe is true even if it's not what everyone wants to hear.
