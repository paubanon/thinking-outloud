It's been some months since I opened the channel, I don't know exactly how many. To mark the I-don't-know-how-manyth one, I was going to make a video asking myself again what I want to achieve with the channel and why I make it. Because if you don't have a clear objective, you're almost always going to feel frustrated with the results. Knowing where you want to go is what keeps you motivated to continue. Or to stop, to consider quitting.

So the channel, after I-don't-know-how-many months, hasn't blown up, the way I expected it might. But it has resonated with not many people — though I think it's resonated strongly with the people it has reached. Based on the type of comments I get and the kind of feedback I receive. It's not "Awesome!" — it's thoughtful comments, deep messages, saying it's resonated a lot with certain people.

And that's what I wanted. I didn't want to be the next most famous YouTuber in Spain. I wanted to share what I think. And that's what led me to open the channel and it's why I keep doing it.

I've also earned a few haters, as you should when you make something of your own. It's a good indicator that you're doing something authentic. If you're not earning haters, you're doing something wrong. Because when you do something authentic it has to go against the norm. And when you go against the norm there are people who won't like it. Doesn't matter.

The channel isn't for gaining a billion followers. It's a tool for myself, to organize my thoughts. Because when you articulate something out loud, you organize them. You see where you fall short and where you can refine. And that's what helps me structure them — more or less linearly, or maybe as a tree, but to structure them.

And instead of doing it just for myself, writing it in a diary and letting it rot until someone a few years after my death decides maybe it's interesting, I've decided it's more interesting — though harder due to the exposure it creates — to put these thoughts within reach of the general public.

So I do it to organize my thoughts, and because I think that, even if it's a small minority, it can help, it can appeal to, it can serve to organize their thoughts too. And knowing this helps me avoid falling into: damn, I wish more people would see it, not enough people have seen it. The likes don't mean much to me. It hasn't reached as many people as it should. Instead of getting frustrated by that, remember why you do it: you do it for yourself, and you do it in a way that if someone decides your thoughts can help them live a better life or reflect on certain aspects of their own, that serves them much better now than when I'm dead.

So, it was just a small reminder for you and for me. See you next time!
