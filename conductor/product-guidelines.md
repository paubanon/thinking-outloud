# Product Guidelines

## Brand Identity
- **Name:** Pensando en Voz Alta / Thinking Out Loud
- **Positioning:** A standalone publication for long-form, unfiltered thought
- **No logo** — the site name in the mechanographic typeface IS the brand

## Tone & Voice
- Neutral and clean — the transcriptions speak for themselves
- Minimal editorial voice on the site itself (navigation labels, UI copy)
- No embellishment, no marketing language, no calls to action
- The content is raw and unedited; the website should feel the same

## Visual Identity
- **Palette:** Black and white only. No accent colors, no gradients
- **Typography:** Mechanographic / typewriter-style font for all text
- **Density:** Spacious — generous whitespace, large readable text, few elements per screen. Like reading a book page
- **Layout:** Content-first. No sidebars, no widgets, no visual clutter

## Navigation
- Ultra-minimal: site name ("Pensando en Voz Alta") top-left as home link
- Language toggle (ES/EN) top-right
- Nothing else in the header — categories accessible from the main content listing
- The reader is never more than one click from any article

## Language & i18n
- **Primary language:** Spanish (original content)
- **Secondary language:** English (translated)
- Auto-detected based on visitor's browser/region locale
- Simple ES/EN toggle always accessible
- English content carries a small, unobtrusive disclaimer: "This is an automatic translation from the original Spanish, respecting the author's tone and voice as closely as possible"
- All UI labels (navigation, filters, search) are localized in both languages

## Content Presentation
- Posts display: title, full transcription text, link to YouTube video
- No truncation or "read more" — long-form by design
- No comments, no social share buttons, no engagement metrics
- The reading experience should feel quiet and focused
